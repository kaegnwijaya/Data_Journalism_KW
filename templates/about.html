Suicides in America

For this project, I chose to investigate the totals of suicides in America from 2014 to 2025. I chose this data set because I thought it would be interesting to compare the suicide rates between the states and to figure out why they may differentiate. This data set comes from the CDC's public webpage for suicide awareness, which highlights the importance of acknowledging mental health and the dangers of suicide.
In order to visualize this data, I wanted to display an infographic in order to show a viewpoint of all America for easier comparisons between states. Below is a prototype of what this infographic would have looked like.
In addition to the large infographic, I created smaller pages that focused on a smaller viewpoint of my data. These viewpoints were represented in pages specifically for a few states which were hand picked for one reason or another.
The prototype for an example of one of these pages can be seen below.
After asking a few of my classmates for some feedback on these prototypes, I recieved a lot of advice. For my main infographic, they suggested that I add a key in order for people to have a general range of what numbers were being worked with. I also recieved adivce to make the top bar the same color as the footer. For my line graph, I recieved advice to add tick marks to the bars and also to choose a color other than gray to represent the total in order to make it look more visually appealing.
Takeaways
  • Add a legend/key to the infographic
  • Make the header and footer the same colors
  • Change color of the gray line
All data from the CDC's public suicide prevention page. This investigation covered the suicide totals in each state from 2014 to 2025, as well as age-adjusted rates for faciliated comparison.
